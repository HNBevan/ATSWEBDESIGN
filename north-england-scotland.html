FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there specific cold weather or winter safety training modules required in the North and Scotland?

Yes. Mandatory winter training covers topics like slips and falls on ice, safe use of de-icing agents, maintaining adequate vehicle breakdown kits, and procedures for accessing sites during extreme weather conditions. This is particularly relevant for businesses with outdoor workers, delivery operations, and rural sites across the North and Scotland.

Does the Scottish Government's regulatory framework affect our standard health and safety training content?

While the core legal duties remain the same (HSE-enforced across Great Britain), training may highlight areas where Scottish regulations or guidance provide specific direction, such as zero-emission zones in city centres. All policies confirm adherence to HSE (GB) standards regardless of location, and our trainers are familiar with any Scottish-specific workplace considerations.

What training is provided regarding work in or near water for ports, docks, and waterways in the North?

For any employee whose duties bring them near docks, rivers, or large bodies of water, mandatory Water Safety Awareness Training and location-specific training on the use of throw lines or life jackets is required. This is particularly relevant for businesses operating near Liverpool's docks, Newcastle's waterfront, and the many coastal and waterway sites across Scotland and the North East.

Can you deliver training to multiple sites across the North and Scotland in the same week?

Yes. We can coordinate multi-site training programmes across the North and Scotland, scheduling our trainers to visit different premises across multiple days or the same week. This is ideal for businesses with multiple branches or facilities spread across the region. Contact us to discuss a bespoke programme and get a consolidated quote.
