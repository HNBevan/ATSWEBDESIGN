1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how you use the site, and improve your experience on return visits.

Cookies cannot harm your device and do not contain personal information such as your name or payment details. You have the right to accept or decline non-essential cookies at any time.

3. Google Analytics & Search Console

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our website. This includes information such as which pages are visited, how long users spend on each page, and which search terms brought visitors to our site.

We also use Google Search Console to monitor our website’s presence in Google Search results. Search Console does not set cookies on your device.

Google Analytics data is:

  • Anonymised - we do not see individual identifiable users
  • Aggregated - we see trends and totals, not individual records
  • Not shared with third parties for advertising or marketing
  • Retained for 26 months in line with Google’s default settings

You can learn more about how Google uses this data at policies.google.com/privacy.

5. Browser Controls

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies stored on your device
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may affect website functionality)
  • Receive a notification before a cookie is stored

Browser-specific guidance:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Apple Safari
